Cosmic phenomens

The average temperature of outer space is 2.7 K (-270.45 °C). But in the depths of space, about 5,000 light years from Earth, there is an even colder region - the Boomerang Nebula. Its temperature is only 1 K (-272.15 °C) - that's just one degree above absolute zero.

To be the center of an entire galaxy and then to be thrown away is a sad fate. But this is exactly what happened to the black hole 3C 186. Scientists suggest that only another black hole is capable of this. The rogue black hole flew more than 35,000 light years from the center of its galaxy to its outskirts. This speed was enough for the black hole to leave its galaxy in 20 million years and set off on an eternal journey through the Universe. And now this piece of singularity is flying in empty space. 3C 186 is the most massive drifting black hole ever seen, weighing more than a billion of our suns combined.

The black hole APM 08279+5255 is surrounded by a monstrous cloud of water vapor. This fog contains 140 trillion times more water than our planet. This cloud is not only the largest, but also the oldest known. It formed when the Universe was only 1.6 billion years old.

TrEs-2b is a very unusual planet. It is a gas giant, but not like our Jupiter: it is slightly larger and black. Absolutely black. The planet's geometric albedo is less than 1%, meaning it reflects less than a percent of its star's light.

Our galaxy, like the Andromeda and other galaxies that make up the so-called Local Group, does not stand still. They are moving towards... something. This something is a gravitational anomaly called the Great Attractor. It is impossible to understand what the Great Attractor is, since it is located almost in the very center of the Avoidance Zone - this is the area of ​​the sky obscured by the disk of the Milky Way. But what is most interesting is that the Great Attractor is not motionless either. It in turn is moving towards the Shapley Supercluster - a huge group of 8,000 galaxies with a mass of more than 10 million billion Suns.

HM Cancri is a double star consisting of two white dwarfs. They revolve around each other at a speed of more than 400 km/s, making a full revolution in 5.4 minutes! However, they are separated by only 80,000 km - 1/5 of the distance from the Earth to the Moon. This is the fastest double star known to us. In about 340 thousand years, the rotation will end and one star will fall onto another. In the meantime, they are getting closer to 60 cm a day.
