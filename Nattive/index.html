What is Space Odyssey?

A Space Odyssey is a free web collection of basic facts about astronomy and cosmology; new images of the nearest corners of our Universe; and simply an educational guide for space lovers

Space Odyssey gives you the opportunity to explore our planet, solar system, galaxy and universe. Provided free of charge, Space Odyssey is developed by a team of educators and communications specialists who collaborate to ensure content is accurate, relevant, engaging, relevant and accessible to a wide audience.

History of the Universe

The universe began to exist more than 13.7 billion years ago as a result of the Big Bang, which occurred at a point of dense and hot singularity. Over time, gravity collected matter into stars and galaxies, within which planets were born. One such planet, Earth, developed life, including humanity, capable of studying and understanding this incredible cosmic history.

Now we are diligently exploring space and everything connected with it. Technology is advancing and this allows people to more effectively explore what has always been mysterious and unknowable. What is hidden millions of light years away from us is what is so interesting to us.

Different forms of light: Explore visible and invisible wavelengths of light that help us understand features like the dusty brim of the Sombrero Galaxy roughly 30 million light-years away.

Hidden objects: Unveil invisible light to reveal hidden objects like the stars forming inside Mystic Mountain, a pillar of gas and dust 7,500 light-years from Earth.

The Hubble Space Telescope was launched in 1990 and remains one of the most famous and important astronomical instruments. It is located in Earth's orbit and is capable of photographing celestial objects in various spectral ranges, allowing astronomers to study planets, stars, galaxies and the depths of the Universe.

The Kepler Space Telescope was designed to detect exoplanets in other star systems. It was launched in 2009 and during its more than nine years of active mission, it collected a huge amount of data, leading to the discovery of thousands of exoplanets, including those in the habitable zone of their stars. Kepler greatly expanded our understanding of the diversity of planets in the Universe.
